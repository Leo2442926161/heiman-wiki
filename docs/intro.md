# Introduction

## About this wiki
This wiki is an **unofficial community-driven** knowledge base for Heiman products.

The content is written and maintained by me based on my personal experience and understanding of Heiman products. It is **not officially reviewed, approved, or endorsed by Heiman** and may contain mistakes, inaccuracies, or outdated information.

But I will update useful docments here.

All original content is written in English. Other language versions are generated with the help of AI translation tools and may contain translation errors.

If you find any issues, inaccuracies, or areas for improvement, please feel free to let me know.


## Why do I maintain this Heiman Wiki?
Official documentation often goes through a long process:

> Draft → Review → Revision → Review → Start over → Review...

This is necessary to ensure accuracy, but it can also make information slower to reach users—especially DIY enthusiasts who enjoy experimenting with new devices and features.

I created this wiki to provide a more accessible and community-friendly resource where users can quickly learn about:

 + Heiman products
 + Supported platforms and ecosystems
 + Matter and Zigbee integrations
 + Troubleshooting guides
 + Tips, tricks, and best practices

While some information here may not be perfect, my goal is to make it practical, useful, and easy to understand.

At Heiman, we don't just want to build great products—we also want users to have a great experience using them. Clear documentation is an important part of that experience.

Hopefully, when you think of smart safety devices, you'll think of **Heiman**, and when you think of Heiman, you'll think of Leo. 😄

#### Supported Technologies
**Matter over Thread**: The latest interoperable smart home standard, offering secure, reliable, and low-power mesh networking.

**Matter over Wi-Fi**: High-performance connectivity for Matter-enabled ecosystems and bridge solutions.

**Zigbee 3.0**: Industry-proven low-power mesh networking technology with broad ecosystem support.

**Z-Wave**: Widely adopted smart home protocol across Europe and North America.

**Wi-Fi**: Direct cloud and local connectivity without requiring a dedicated gateway.

**Sub-GHz (Sub-1G)**: Ultra-long-range, low-power wireless communication designed for professional safety application
